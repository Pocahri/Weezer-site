Weezer(The Blue Album)

Produced by former Cars frontman Ric Ocasek and recorded in Electric Lady Studios in New York City, Weezer released their debut album on May 10, 1994 through Geffen Records. With popular singles "Undone - The Sweater Song" and "Buddy Holly", Weezer found mainstream success. Blue Album became a certified triple-platinum in the US and a double-platinum in Canada.

Make Believe

Released on May 10, 2005 through Geffen Records. This album was Weezer's biggest char album peaks yet, hitting #2 in the US, #1 in Canada, and #11 in the UK. It eventually hit platinum status. Beverly Hills was the bands first Grammy nomination, nominated for Best Rock Song. Despite this, Make Believe received mixed reviews from critics and fans; however, it has remained a consistant seller.
