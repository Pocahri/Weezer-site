Lead guitarist Rivers Cuomo, drummer Patrick Wilson, bassist Matt Sharp, and guitarist Jason Cropper founded Weezer in Los Angeles in 1992. During the recording of The Blue Album Cropper left and was replaced by guitarist Brain Bell.

After the release of Pinkerton Weezer went on a hiatus. During this time, Sharp left the group in April 1998 and was replaced by Mikey Welsh.

Weezer did not reunite until April 200 when Fuji Rock Festival in Japan offered Weezer a gig to play in August 2000. Eventually, the band went back to produce the third album, The Green Album. After the album's release, Welsh left the band and was replaced by Scott Shriner.

Members

Rivers Cuomo

Born in Manhattan, NY on June 13, 1970 to parents of Italian and German descent. He was raised on an ashram run by the late yoga master Sri Swami Satchidananda in Pomfert, Connecticut. During his early childhood, Cuomo attended a private school on the ashram where his parents raised him and his brother Leaves. Cuomo attented E.O. Smith High School in Storrs, Connecticut under the name Peter Kitts. He graduated from Harvard University with Cum Laude and Phi Beta Kappa.
He is married to Kyoko Ito and has two children; one daugther and one son.

Brian Bell

Born in Iowa City, Iowa on December 9, 1968 and raised in Knoxville, Tennessee, Bell gravitated to music at the age of four when his parents took him to an Elvis Presley concert. Bell was forced by his mother to take piano lessons, and refused to let him take guitar lessons until high school because didn't believe he would practice. During his freshman year, his parents finally allowed him to take guitar lessons.
After graduating high school, Bell decided not to pursue higher education feeling it would be a "waste of money". In 1991, he became a member of Carnival Art, which released three offical albums with Bell playing bass. However, due to poor reception, Carnival Art was later dropped by the record label. Later, he quit and joined Weezer.

Patrick Wilson

Born in Buffalo, New York on February 1, 1969 and raised in nearby Clarence. Wilson was introduced to music very early on in life. After attending his first concert, he was inspired to learn to play the drums.
After graduating high school in 1987, Wilson briefly attended a local college; he dropped out after one semester. He commented, "...College is great if you want to learn, but that's not what college is about, it's about making your professor happy and getting good grades and getting into IBM. Any place that says that they're only accepting college graduates is not a place I'm very interested in being." At 21, he moved to Los Angeles and met Cuomo while playing for different bands.

Scott Shriner

Born in Toledo, Ohio in July 11, 1965. He took up bass in high school. After being discharged from the Marine Corps, he studied bass with his high school teacher, Mark Kieswetter until moving to Los Angeles in 1989. At 25 he attended Musicians Institute and went to play on several bands, most notably, Vanilla Ice's backing band.
